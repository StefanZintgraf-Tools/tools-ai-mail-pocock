# Glossary — ubiquitous language

One canonical term per concept, so code, schemas, and docs speak the same
language. The **Vision phrasings** column lists the various ways the vision (which
is deliberately non-technical) names the same thing — treat those as synonyms of
the canonical term, not as distinct concepts.

| Canonical term | Definition | Vision phrasings it absorbs |
|----------------|------------|-----------------------------|
| **Message** | A single email or chat message. | "email", "message", "note", "letter" |
| **Thread** | A connected back-and-forth on one matter. | "thread", "exchange", "the endless thread", "conversation" |
| **Channel** | A source the assistant reads/acts in (an email account or a chat app). | "chat apps", "WhatsApp, Telegram and the rest", "my inbox" |
| **Account** | One mailbox/channel the user owns and connects. | "private inbox", "work inbox", "side-project addresses" |
| **Person (unified identity)** | One human, recognised as the same individual across all their addresses and channels. | "it's all one person", "the supplier who emails and also messages me", "a single thread of a relationship" |
| **Relationship memory** | The accumulated context about a Person across years and sources. | "remember the context across years", "the full picture", "how we know each other" |
| **Briefing** | The short daily "what actually needs you today" summary. | "short briefing", "a handful of things that actually need you" |
| **Set-aside** | A non-destructive holding place: items moved out of the active inbox but kept, visible, and restorable (never deleted — see INV2). | "quiet read-only corner", "for your information, nothing needed", "set aside and flagged", "held quietly at the door" |
| **Draft** | A proposed reply/message prepared for the user, awaiting approval (INV1). | "the reply already drafted", "a real first draft", "drafted in my own voice" |
| **Voice** | The user's personal writing style, reproduced in drafts. | "in my own voice", "the way I'd really send it" |
| **Commitment** | An obligation or deadline inferred from correspondence — owed by the user or owed *to* them. | "deadlines and obligations", "loose ends", "you said you'd send", "the reply I'm still owed" |
| **Open thread** | A conversation still in flight, with a side whose turn it is. | "open thread", "which balls are still in the air", "whose court they're in", "in limbo" |
| **Nudge** | A proactive prompt the assistant raises (cold relationship, overdue reply, looming miss). | "nudge", "heads-up", "want me to draft something?" |
| **Money-trap flag** | A fraud/scam signal raised from knowing the user's normal (forged invoice, changed bank details, wire pressure). | "money traps", "forged invoice", "we've changed our bank details", "urgent-money traps" |
| **Document store** | Where the user keeps documents, kept in sync with mail attachments. | "the place I already keep my documents", "my filing", "my document store" |
| **Knowledge base** | A company's store of solved answers / reference material, consulted when past mail doesn't answer. | "our own knowledge base", "how we solved them before", "the spec everyone refers to" |
| **Autonomy boundary** | The per-context policy of what the assistant may do on its own vs. must ask first (INV6). | "how careful the assistant is", "where it may simply go ahead", "a longer leash", "the boundary is mine to set" |
| **Activity record** | The persisted, explained, auditable log of what the assistant did and why (INV3 + INV4). | "show me everything it did and why", "a complete, lasting record of every decision" |
| **Delegated exchange** | A multi-round conversation the assistant carries on the user's behalf toward a result, surfacing only for decisions/approval. | "the whole negotiation happen in the background", "carry a whole exchange to its finish", "chase the supplier across the three rounds" |
| **Shared inbox** | A mailbox several people work out of together. | "our shared inbox", "the same inbox", "shared mailboxes" |
| **Assignment / routing** | Directing a message to the right person (by team convention, availability, or expertise). | "files each message into the right person's place", "steering it to whoever's covering", "point it to the right specialist" |
| **Handoff** | Passing a conversation to another person with its full context attached. | "passing a conversation to a colleague", "arrive already understood", "travels with the message" |
| **Collaborator (ad-hoc)** | Someone pulled into a thread temporarily, then dropped back out, without a full handoff. | "pull a colleague in for just this moment", "quietly drops them back out" |
| **Cover / availability** | Who is reachable vs. away (holiday, sick, travel), used for routing. | "who's actually reachable", "whoever's covering" |
| **History backfill** | Learning from the user's pre-existing archive so the assistant is useful from day one. | "useful from the very first day", "learned from everything that's already there", "the years I've already piled up" |
| **Doorkeeper** | First-contact triage for a public inbox: real enquiry vs. mass pitch. | "act as a doorkeeper", "the real first-contact worth my time", "the generic mass pitch" |
| **Send-time check** | A pre-send safeguard intercepting mistakes at the moment before sending. | "catch me in the very moment before I send", "this is going to two hundred people, sure?" |
